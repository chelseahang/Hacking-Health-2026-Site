Problem Space 03 — Checklist

Inventory Sales Pipeline Optimization

⏱ Total Time: ~45 minutes 🎯 6 Steps 🏆 100 Points

Test your agent directly to validate it meets the judging criteria. Each step contains prompts to paste directly into your agent and checklists to track your score.

📋 Process Overview

Each step contains prompts to paste directly into your agent. The goal is to test whether your agent responds accurately, intelligently, and in line with the problem it was built to solve. Complete the checklists after each response to track your score.

Step 1
Business Impact Assessment
⏱ 10 minutes  ·  25 points
🎯 Purpose Validate your understanding of the business problem before testing your agent.
Research & Resources — Review Before Testing
  • Current inventory and sellout data flows across wholesalers, distributors, and retail partners
  • How long it currently takes for data to reach commercial, supply chain, and brand teams (weeks to months)
  • Key stakeholders and their specific visibility needs
  • Past product launches where delayed insights impacted performance
  • Revenue impact of stockouts and oversupply events
Self-Assessment — Can You Answer These Clearly?
  • What specific blind spots exist today during product launches?
  • How long does it currently take to detect a stockout or oversupply issue?
  • Which teams are most impacted and what decisions are they unable to make?
  • What does "real-time" mean in this context — hourly, daily, weekly?
  • What is the revenue cost of a missed stockout during a product launch?
⚠️ If you can't answer these clearly, your agent won't either.

✅ Checklist — Business Value Foundation (25 points)

  • Deep understanding of current data fragmentation across distribution network
  • Clear articulation of blind spots during product launches
  • Identified key user personas (commercial, supply chain, brand teams)
  • Understood the cost of reactive vs. proactive decision-making
  • Defined what "real-time visibility" means in operational terms
Step 2
Data Ingestion & Standardization Validation
⏱ 12 minutes  ·  20 points
🧠 Purpose Test whether your agent handles fragmented, multi-source data intelligently.
Prompt 2A: Multi-Source Ingestion
Paste this prompt into your agent
We receive inventory data from 3 wholesalers, 5 distributors, and
12 retail partners. Each sends data in different formats — some via 
EDI, some via Excel, some via API. Wholesaler A sends data daily, 
Distributor B sends weekly, and Retail Partner C sends monthly. 
How is this data ingested, standardized, and unified into a single view?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent describe specific ingestion approaches for each format (EDI, Excel, API)?
  • Does it explain how different data cadences are handled?
  • Does it describe standardization logic (mapping different field names, units, SKUs)?
  • Does it explain how gaps in data (monthly vs. daily) are handled?
  • Does it describe how conflicts between sources are resolved?
  • Red flag: Assumes all data arrives in the same format or cadence
Prompt 2B: Data Quality & Gaps
Paste this prompt into your agent
Wholesaler B hasn't sent data in 10 days. Distributor C sent data
with 200 missing SKU codes. Retail Partner D sent inventory figures 
that are 40% lower than last week with no explanation. How are 
these data quality issues detected, flagged, and handled?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent detect the 10-day data gap from Wholesaler B?
  • Does it flag the 200 missing SKU codes from Distributor C?
  • Does it identify the 40% drop from Retail Partner D as anomalous?
  • Does it describe fallback strategies for each scenario?
  • Does it alert the right teams about data quality issues?
  • Red flag: Silently accepts bad or missing data without flagging
Prompt 2C: Real-Time vs. Delayed Data
Paste this prompt into your agent
Some partners send real-time data via API, others send weekly batch
files. A product launch is happening tomorrow and we need current 
stock levels across all channels. How does the system handle the 
mix of real-time and delayed data sources and what confidence level 
can we assign to the inventory view?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent distinguish between real-time and delayed data sources?
  • Does it assign confidence levels or data freshness indicators?
  • Does it explain how stale data is handled during critical events (launches)?
  • Does it suggest how to fill gaps with estimates or forecasts?
  • Does it communicate data freshness clearly to users?
  • Red flag: Presents all data as equally current regardless of source

✅ Checklist — Data Ingestion & Standardization (20 points)

  • Handles multiple data formats (EDI, Excel, API) with specific approaches
  • Manages different data cadences (daily, weekly, monthly) intelligently
  • Detects and flags data quality issues (gaps, anomalies, missing fields)
  • Assigns data freshness and confidence indicators
  • Resolves conflicts between data sources with clear logic
  • Handles critical events (launches) with best available data
Step 3
Inventory & Sellout Intelligence
⏱ 8 minutes  ·  20 points
🌍 Purpose Test whether your agent delivers actionable inventory and sellout insights.
Prompt 3A: Stockout Risk Detection
Paste this prompt into your agent
Product X was launched 3 weeks ago. Current stock levels across
the distribution network: Region A has 2 weeks of supply remaining, 
Region B has 5 days, Region C has 6 weeks, and Region D has no data 
for 7 days. Sellout velocity has increased 30% week-over-week. 
Which regions are at risk of stockout and what should we do?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent correctly identify Region B as critical (5 days)?
  • Does it flag Region A as at-risk given 30% velocity increase?
  • Does it flag Region D as unknown risk due to missing data?
  • Does it recommend specific actions (reallocation, expedited shipment)?
  • Does it factor in the velocity increase when calculating risk?
  • Red flag: Only flags Region B without considering velocity trends
Prompt 3B: Sellout Trend Analysis
Paste this prompt into your agent
Show me week-over-week sellout trends for Product Y by channel
(pharmacy, hospital, specialty) over the last 8 weeks. Pharmacy 
channel is up 15%, hospital is flat, and specialty is down 20%. 
What does this tell us and what actions should be taken?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent correctly interpret the channel-level trends?
  • Does it identify the specialty channel decline as a concern?
  • Does it suggest root cause analysis for the specialty decline?
  • Does it recommend channel-specific actions?
  • Does it connect trends to field force or promotional strategy?
  • Red flag: Reports numbers without providing actionable interpretation
Prompt 3C: Oversupply Detection
Paste this prompt into your agent
Product Z has been on the market for 6 months. Region E has
18 weeks of supply but sellout velocity has dropped 40% over 
the last month. Region F has 12 weeks of supply with stable 
velocity. What are the risks and what inventory reallocation 
would you recommend?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent identify Region E as an oversupply risk?
  • Does it calculate the effective weeks of supply given reduced velocity?
  • Does it distinguish between Region E (high risk) and Region F (stable)?
  • Does it recommend specific reallocation actions?
  • Does it flag potential expiry or write-off risks?
  • Red flag: Treats both regions the same or misses the velocity impact

✅ Checklist — Inventory & Sellout Intelligence (20 points)

  • Correctly identifies stockout risks using stock levels AND velocity
  • Detects oversupply risks and recommends reallocation
  • Provides channel-level sellout trend analysis
  • Connects inventory insights to actionable recommendations
  • Factors in data gaps when assessing risk
  • Goes beyond reporting numbers to providing strategic guidance
Step 4
Production Readiness & Scalability
⏱ 8 minutes  ·  15 points
⚙️ Purpose Test whether your agent handles real-world operational challenges at scale.
Prompt 4A: Product Launch Readiness
Paste this prompt into your agent
A new product launches in 48 hours across 8 regions and 3 channels.
We need to monitor stock levels, sellout velocity, and distribution 
coverage in real-time. What does the launch monitoring dashboard 
show and what alerts are triggered if something goes wrong?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent describe a launch-specific monitoring view?
  • Does it define alert thresholds for stockout risk?
  • Does it describe real-time vs. near-real-time monitoring cadence?
  • Does it identify who gets alerted and how?
  • Does it describe escalation paths for critical issues?
  • Red flag: No launch-specific monitoring or alerting capability
Prompt 4B: Multi-Product & Multi-Market Scaling
Paste this prompt into your agent
We currently track 5 products across Canada. We want to scale to
25 products across Canada, US, and EU. Each market has different 
distribution partners, data formats, and reporting cadences. 
How does the system scale and what changes are needed?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent address multi-product scaling without performance degradation?
  • Does it explain how new markets and partners are onboarded?
  • Does it address different regulatory and data requirements by market?
  • Does it describe how the data model scales?
  • Does it identify potential bottlenecks?
  • Red flag: No mention of scaling challenges or onboarding new partners
Prompt 4C: Workflow Integration
Paste this prompt into your agent
A supply chain manager needs to see stockout alerts in their
existing planning tool. A brand manager needs weekly sellout 
reports in PowerPoint. A commercial director needs a real-time 
dashboard on their phone. How does the system serve all three 
users without requiring them to change their workflows?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent describe role-specific output formats?
  • Does it explain integration with existing planning tools?
  • Does it describe automated report generation?
  • Does it address mobile accessibility?
  • Does it minimize workflow disruption for each user?
  • Red flag: One-size-fits-all output that doesn't adapt to user needs
Prompt 4D: Data Governance & Compliance
Paste this prompt into your agent
Our distribution partners have strict data sharing agreements.
Some data can only be used for internal planning, not shared 
externally. Some markets have data residency requirements. 
How is data governance enforced and how are partner agreements 
respected?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent describe data access controls by partner?
  • Does it address data residency requirements by market?
  • Does it explain how usage restrictions are enforced?
  • Does it describe audit trails for data access?
  • Does it demonstrate awareness of partner agreement complexity?
  • Red flag: No mention of data governance or partner restrictions

✅ Checklist — Production Readiness (15 points)

  • Launch-specific monitoring and alerting capability
  • Scales to multiple products and markets without performance issues
  • Serves different user personas with role-appropriate outputs
  • Integrates with existing planning and reporting workflows
  • Enforces data governance and partner agreement restrictions
  • Responses are operationally actionable, not theoretical
Step 5
Innovation & Technical Excellence
⏱ 5 minutes  ·  15 points
🛠️ Purpose Test the depth, adaptability, and intelligence of your agent.
Prompt 5A: Adaptive Intelligence
Paste this prompt into your agent
Over the last 3 product launches, stockout alerts were triggered
too late — on average 4 days after the stockout began. The pattern: 
regions with weekly data feeds are always late. Regions with daily 
feeds are fine. How should the alerting model be adjusted and what 
can be done for weekly-feed regions?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent identify the weekly feed cadence as the root cause?
  • Does it suggest predictive alerting based on velocity trends?
  • Does it recommend interim solutions for weekly-feed regions?
  • Does it propose model adjustments with specific logic?
  • Does it demonstrate learning from historical patterns?
  • Red flag: Identifies the problem but offers no specific solution
Prompt 5B: Conversational Intelligence
Paste this prompt into your agent
Which regions are at risk of stockout for Product X in the next
14 days, factoring in current stock levels, week-over-week sellout 
velocity trends, and any upcoming promotional events that could 
spike demand?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent handle a complex, multi-variable query?
  • Does it factor in stock levels, velocity trends, AND promotional events?
  • Does it provide a ranked list of at-risk regions?
  • Does it explain the reasoning for each region's risk level?
  • Does it recommend specific actions per region?
  • Red flag: Only considers one variable (stock level) without velocity or events
Prompt 5C: Predictive Capability
Paste this prompt into your agent
Based on sellout velocity trends for the first 4 weeks of launch,
Product Y is tracking 25% above forecast in pharmacy and 15% below 
in hospital. If these trends continue, what will inventory levels 
look like in 6 weeks and what supply adjustments should be made now?
What to Look For in the Response
  • Does the agent project inventory levels 6 weeks out?
  • Does it factor in channel-specific velocity differences?
  • Does it recommend specific supply adjustments?
  • Does it quantify the risk of inaction?
  • Does it go beyond reporting to proactive supply planning?
  • Red flag: No forward-looking projection or supply recommendation

✅ Checklist — Technical Excellence (15 points)

  • Adapts alerting model based on historical patterns and feedback
  • Handles complex multi-variable queries (stock + velocity + events)
  • Demonstrates predictive capability for proactive supply planning
  • Maintains response quality across varied query types
  • Goes beyond reporting numbers to strategic, forward-looking insights
