    Problem Spaces

    The health challenges you'll be tackling.

    01

    Commercial Canada: Invoice – Pricing – Revenue Reconciliation

    Commercial and Market Access teams in Canada manually validate thousands of payer invoices annually, creating significant resource strain and delays in strategic insights. Current processes rely on external consultants, costing $200K–$300K annually, while limiting real-time visibility into $100M+ in revenue.

    The challenge: build an AI solution that automatically ingests, validates, and analyzes invoices (PDF + Excel), applies agreement-specific rules, and enables conversational queries across historical data — creating a scalable template for global invoice reconciliation.

    02

    HCP Consent Prioritization for Concierge for Field (Jarvis)

    Under Canada's CASL regulations, commercial teams have one meaningful opportunity to request digital consent from healthcare professionals (HCPs). With thousands of HCPs currently non-consented and outreach largely undifferentiated, limited field force resources are being applied broadly rather than strategically — risking missed opportunities with high-value prescribers.

    The challenge: build a data-driven prioritization engine that scores non-consented HCPs using CRM engagement data and IQVIA prescriber insights, classifies them into priority tiers, and recommends tailored consent strategies — maximizing field force ROI and creating a repeatable framework scalable across Canada, EU, Australia, and Asia.

    03

    Metadata Explorer

    Business and medical teams struggle to extract timely insights from fragmented data sources, relying on static dashboards and siloed datasets that create barriers between data and decision-making.

    The challenge: build a platform that enables external vendors to share reports through a simple, single-link mechanism with automated standardization, while empowering teams to query insights conversationally via Concierge — breaking down data silos and accelerating decision-making across business and medical functions.

    04

    COPD Care Gaps RWD / Cure Health (Care Gaps for Atopic Dermatitis – Dupixent)

    Real-world evidence (RWE) analysis for Canadian data currently relies on manual, fragmented approaches that fail to surface actionable insights at the speed required for strategic decision-making across R&D and commercial teams.

    The challenge: build an agentic workflow that continuously processes real-world data sources to identify patterns in treatment outcomes, patient journeys, and market dynamics — surfacing insights that inform both R&D and commercial strategies with the speed and depth needed for competitive advantage.
