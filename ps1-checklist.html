Commercial Invoice to Revenue Accuracy

🔍 Agent Verification Process

Test your agent directly to validate it meets the judging criteria.

Total Time: ~45 minutes  •  Purpose: Test your agent directly to validate it meets the judging criteria

Each step contains prompts to paste directly into your agent. The goal is to test whether your agent responds accurately, intelligently, and in line with the problem it was built to solve. Complete the checklists after each response to track your score.

Step 1: Business Impact Assessment

⏱ 10 minutes 🎯 Validate your understanding of the business problem

Research & Resources

Before testing your agent, review:

  • Current Commercial Canada invoice validation process and workflows
  • Payer agreement structures and pricing models
  • Key stakeholders: Trade & Revenue Management, Market Access, PSP, Finance
  • Time spent on manual reconciliation (hours/week per analyst)
  • Current consultant costs ($200K–$300K annually) and revenue impact (~$100M+)

Self-Assessment

Can you answer these clearly?

  • What is the ~$100M+ revenue impact in specific terms?
  • Why is manual validation a bottleneck?
  • What does a Trade & Revenue Management analyst’s typical day look like?
  • How much time would your solution save?
  • How does it eliminate the $200K–$300K consultant cost?

If you can’t answer these clearly, your agent won’t either.

✅ Checklist — Business Value Foundation (25 points)

Step 2: Reconciliation Intelligence Validation

⏱ 12 minutes 🧠 Test whether your agent’s reconciliation logic is accurate, explainable, and actionable

Prompts to test

Prompt 2A — Document Processing Logic 
Invoices arrive as scanned PDFs with inconsistent layouts and also
as structured Excel files. Some PDFs are corrupted, some Excel files
have missing columns, and about 15% of all invoices have data quality
issues. How are these handled during ingestion?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent describe specific PDF parsing approaches?
  • ✅ Does it explain Excel data extraction and validation?
  • ✅ Does it address the 15% data quality issue with fallback strategies?
  • ✅ Does it explain validation rules applied during ingestion?
  • ✅ Does it describe how corrupted or incomplete data is flagged?
  • ❌ Red flag: Vague response like “we handle both formats”
Prompt 2B — Agreement-Specific Rules 
Payer A has a 15% volume discount on Drug X for orders over 1,000 units.
Payer B uses tiered pricing with different rates per 500-unit tier.
Invoice from Payer A shows 1,200 units at full price ($50,000) but
should be $42,500 with discount. Invoice from Payer B shows 2,500 units
at $40,000 but tiered pricing should be $38,000. How are these
discrepancies detected?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent calculate the correct expected amounts ($42,500 and $38,000)?
  • ✅ Does it identify both discrepancies ($7,500 and $2,000)?
  • ✅ Does it explain how payer-specific rules are applied?
  • ✅ Does it show understanding of volume discounts and tiered pricing?
  • ✅ Does it explain the detection logic step-by-step?
  • ❌ Red flag: Incorrect calculations or missing discrepancies
Prompt 2C — Discrepancy Classification 
Three invoices are flagged: Invoice #4521 shows $50,000 for Drug Y
but agreement says $45,000 (pricing error). Invoice #4522 is missing
3 line items that were shipped per records (missing items). Invoice
#4523 includes a retroactive price adjustment from last quarter
(legitimate variance). How are these classified and prioritized?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent distinguish between error types (pricing, missing items, adjustments)?
  • ✅ Does it classify the retroactive adjustment as legitimate, not an error?
  • ✅ Does it explain severity assessment for each type?
  • ✅ Does it describe prioritization logic?
  • ✅ Does it show understanding of what constitutes an error vs. variance?
  • ❌ Red flag: Treats all discrepancies the same or misclassifies the adjustment
Prompt 2D — Auditability 
Finance manager questions why Invoice #12345 was flagged for a $5,000
discrepancy. The invoice shows $50,000 for Drug Y, the payer agreement
specifies $45,000, and the calculation is $50,000 - $45,000 = $5,000.
Provide the complete audit trail and explanation.

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent provide step-by-step reconciliation logic?
  • ✅ Does it show the data used (invoice amount, agreement terms)?
  • ✅ Does it show the calculation clearly?
  • ✅ Does it provide an audit trail (what was checked, in what order)?
  • ✅ Is the explanation clear enough for Finance to verify?
  • ❌ Red flag: Black box response that can’t explain the decision

✅ Checklist — Reconciliation Intelligence (20 points)

Step 3: Scalability & Global Readiness

⏱ 8 minutes 🌍 Test whether your agent can scale beyond Canada

Prompts to test

Prompt 3A — Multi-Market Adaptation 
US payer structures differ from Canada. HMOs negotiate different
discounts, PPOs use different fee schedules, Medicare has fixed rates,
and Medicaid varies by state. How would the reconciliation rules
adapt for these different US payer models?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent identify specific differences in US payer models?
  • ✅ Does it explain how reconciliation logic would adapt?
  • ✅ Does it address regulatory or compliance differences?
  • ✅ Does it show understanding of payer diversity?
  • ✅ Does it position the solution as adaptable, not Canada-specific?
  • ❌ Red flag: Generic response that doesn’t address market-specific differences
Prompt 3B — Volume Scaling 
Currently processing 5,000 invoices per month in Canada. Scaling
globally to 50,000 invoices per month across multiple markets.
Processing time per invoice is currently 2 minutes. At 10x volume,
what happens to performance and how is this managed?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent address performance considerations at scale?
  • ✅ Does it explain how processing time would scale?
  • ✅ Does it mention infrastructure or architectural changes?
  • ✅ Does it address data storage and retrieval optimization?
  • ✅ Does it demonstrate understanding of scalability challenges?
  • ❌ Red flag: No mention of performance or scaling considerations
Prompt 3C — Data Source Adaptability 
In Canada we use ERP System X and have access to IQVIA data. In
Australia, ERP System X isn't available and IQVIA data is limited.
What alternative data sources could maintain reconciliation quality
and how would accuracy be affected?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent identify alternative data sources?
  • ✅ Does it explain how reconciliation quality would be maintained?
  • ✅ Does it describe fallback strategies?
  • ✅ Does it acknowledge trade-offs honestly?
  • ✅ Does it show flexibility in the framework?
  • ❌ Red flag: Assumes data sources will always be available

✅ Checklist — Scalability & Reach (20 points)

Step 4: Production Readiness & Data Integrity

⏱ 8 minutes ⚙️ Test whether your agent handles real-world operational challenges

Prompts to test

Prompt 4A — Data Freshness 
Invoice data is updated daily from the ERP system. Payer agreement
data is updated monthly. Today is March 15th and we're reconciling
invoices from March 1-14. A payer changed their pricing on March 10th
but the agreement data won't update until March 31st. How is this
handled and what is the impact on reconciliation accuracy?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent acknowledge the data freshness issue?
  • ✅ Does it explain how stale agreement data impacts accuracy?
  • ✅ Does it describe mechanisms to prevent using outdated data?
  • ✅ Does it flag the March 10–14 invoices for manual review?
  • ✅ Does it communicate data freshness to users?
  • ❌ Red flag: No mention of data freshness or update cadence
Prompt 4B — Missing Data Handling 
15% of invoices have incomplete data: missing line items (5%),
unclear payer information (7%), corrupted files (3%). An invoice
is missing 3 line items but we have shipping records showing they
were delivered. Another invoice has no payer ID. How are these
handled and what is the reconciliation outcome?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent acknowledge the 15% data quality issue?
  • ✅ Does it describe fallback strategies for each scenario?
  • ✅ Does it explain how reconciliation quality degrades gracefully?
  • ✅ Does it flag incomplete records for manual review?
  • ✅ Does it maintain transparency about data limitations?
  • ❌ Red flag: Assumes all data will be complete and clean
Prompt 4C — Compliance Guardrails 
Show the reconciliation history for Payer X invoices from Q3 2025.
Include which invoices were flagged, why they were flagged, what
data was used, who reviewed them, and what action was taken. What
compliance requirements does this audit trail meet?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent provide a complete audit trail?
  • ✅ Does it explain data governance and security measures?
  • ✅ Does it demonstrate compliance awareness?
  • ✅ Does it show how data is protected and accessed?
  • ✅ Does it address regulatory requirements?
  • ❌ Red flag: No mention of compliance or data governance
Prompt 4D — Workflow Integration 
A Finance analyst needs to: (1) see flagged invoices, (2) understand
why they were flagged, (3) approve or dispute the flag, (4) export
results to their ERP system. How does this workflow work without
switching between multiple systems?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent describe integration with existing Finance tools?
  • ✅ Does it explain how findings are actioned (exported, flagged, escalated)?
  • ✅ Does it minimize context switching between systems?
  • ✅ Does it address adoption barriers?
  • ✅ Does it describe the user experience?
  • ❌ Red flag: No mention of workflow integration or user experience

✅ Checklist — Production Readiness (15 points)

Step 5: Innovation & Technical Excellence

⏱ 5 minutes 🛠️ Test the depth, adaptability, and intelligence of your agent

Prompts to test

Prompt 5A — Adaptive Learning 
Over 6 months, 40% of flagged discrepancies turned out to be legitimate
variances that should have been accepted. The pattern: invoices from
Payer X with retroactive adjustments are being incorrectly flagged as
errors. What does this tell you and how should the rules be adjusted?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent recognize the pattern in the feedback?
  • ✅ Does it suggest specific rule adjustments?
  • ✅ Does it explain how to validate improvements?
  • ✅ Does it demonstrate learning capability?
  • ✅ Does it propose a feedback loop for continuous improvement?
  • ❌ Red flag: Static response that doesn’t adapt to feedback
Prompt 5B — Conversational Intelligence 
Show me all invoices from Payer X in Q3 where pricing doesn't match
our agreement, calculate the total financial impact, identify which
discrepancies are most likely to be disputed based on historical
patterns, and recommend next steps.

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent handle a complex, multi-part query?
  • ✅ Does it filter by payer and time period correctly?
  • ✅ Does it calculate financial impact accurately?
  • ✅ Does it identify patterns in historical disputes?
  • ✅ Does it provide specific, actionable insights?
  • ❌ Red flag: Can only handle simple, single-part queries
Prompt 5C — Predictive Capability 
Based on historical invoice patterns, Payer X submits invoices with
discrepancies 60% of the time, Payer Y 20% of the time. Payer X's
discrepancies average $15,000, Payer Y's average $2,000. Which payers
are most likely to submit discrepancies next quarter and what should
we do proactively?

What to look for

  • ✅ Does the agent identify patterns in historical data?
  • ✅ Does it make predictions based on those patterns?
  • ✅ Does it suggest proactive actions?
  • ✅ Does it explain the reasoning behind predictions?
  • ✅ Does it go beyond basic reconciliation to strategic insights?
  • ❌ Red flag: No predictive capability, only reactive reconciliation

✅ Checklist — Technical Excellence (15 points)

⏱ 2 minutes 🎤 Create compelling presentation materials for your pitch

Prompt 6 — Hackathon Pitch Presentation 
Create a compelling hackathon pitch presentation for a Commercial
Canada Invoice – Pricing - Revenue Reconciliation solution.

Key details to include:
- Problem: Manual validation of thousands of invoices annually
  affecting ~$100M+ revenue, $200K–$300K external consultant costs,
  days/weeks for insights, limited real-time visibility into
  payer performance
- Solution: "Talk to data" capability that automatically ingests,
  validates, and analyzes invoices (PDF + Excel), applies
  agreement-specific rules, and enables conversational queries
  across historical data
- Impact: Eliminates $200K–$300K consultant costs, accelerates
  insights from days/weeks to minutes, improves revenue accuracy,
  creates scalable global template
- Users: Trade & Revenue Management, Market Access, PSP, Finance

Create 8-10 slides covering: problem statement, solution overview,
technical approach (PDF/Excel ingestion, rule engine, conversational AI),
business impact, demo preview, scalability, implementation roadmap,
and ask.

Target audience: Hackathon judges evaluating business value (25pts),
reconciliation intelligence (20pts), scalability (20pts), production
readiness (15pts), technical excellence (15pts), storytelling (5pts).

Make it visually engaging with clear value propositions, realistic
business impact, and technical depth prominently featured.
